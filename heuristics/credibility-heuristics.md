# 12 Credibility Heuristics

Named rules that determine whether a resume bullet survives a recruiter follow-up. Each heuristic has a pass/fail test.

These are the operational version of the scoring rubric's Credibility dimension.

---

## H1 — Name an artifact

**Rule:** Every bullet should name something a recruiter could ask a follow-up question about.

**Pass test:** "What were the three services?" / "Which dashboard?" — answerable in 30s.

**Fail example:** "Improved system reliability."

**Pass example:** "Rewrote retry logic in the payments worker."

---

## H2 — Prefer concrete verbs over corporate verbs

**Pass:** Built, Wrote, Split, Migrated, Owned, Carried.

**Fail:** Leveraged, Utilized, Spearheaded, Orchestrated, Revolutionized.

---

## H3 — One claim per bullet

**Fail:** Three claims stacked in one 40-word bullet.

**Pass:** One artifact per bullet; split if needed.

---

## H4 — Mixed rhythm

**Fail:** Five bullets, all 22 words, all "Verb + noun + outcome."

**Pass:** One ~10-word bullet, one ~26-word bullet, varied lead words.

---

## H5 — No invented metrics

**Pass:** Number appears in a dashboard, doc, or ticket the candidate can pull up.

**Fail:** "I think it was around 40%."

**Action:** Delete the number or anchor it to a named source.

---

## H6 — Honest scope verbs

Match contribution level: Used < Contributed to < Co-led < Led < Owned < Designed < Founded.

**Fail:** "Led migration" when one of five ICs.

---

## H7 — Length cap (~10% growth on rewrite)

Rewrites should not balloon. Shorter is often correct.

---

## H8 — Survive the six-second scan

Top bullet of most recent role = strongest. First two words of every bullet = concrete.

---

## H9 — Survive one follow-up

For each bullet, write the follow-up question mentally. No answer → fix or delete.

---

## H10 — Ground-truth cross-check

Off-resume notes: what you actually did. Every resume claim must appear in ground truth or be downgraded.

---

## H11 — Unflattering specifics build trust

"Mentioning Q3 outage" / "killed the nav model after testing" — recruiters trust these.

---

## H12 — Skills as searchable tokens

**Fail:** "Proficient in cutting-edge technologies."

**Pass:** "AWS (Lambda, RDS), Python, Terraform."

---

## Quick application

Run H1, H5, H6, H9 on every bullet on page 1. If any fail → Credibility score capped at 6.
